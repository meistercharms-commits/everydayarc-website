# Everyday Arc Brand Identity

## Logo: Stepping Stones

The Everyday Arc icon is three ascending rounded squares, each slightly larger and more prominent than the last. They climb diagonally from bottom-left to top-right, gently rotated to feel organic rather than rigid.

### What it represents

The stepping stones embody the company's core belief: that meaningful progress happens through small, accumulative steps rather than dramatic leaps. Each stone is a quiet moment of growth.

- **Three ascending forms** represent a gentle upward arc, the everyday kind of progress that compounds over time
- **Growing size and opacity** suggest building confidence and presence, starting small and becoming more sure-footed
- **Soft rotation on each stone** prevents the mark from feeling mechanical or clinical, reflecting the human, imperfect nature of real life
- **Rounded corners** signal approachability and safety, reinforcing the psychological safety that sits at the heart of every product

The icon works as both a visual metaphor for the company name (an arc made of everyday steps) and a reflection of its philosophy: meet people where they are, and let them move at their own pace.

### Construction

```
Stone 1 (smallest): 16x16, border-radius 4, rotated -5 degrees
Stone 2 (middle):   20x20, border-radius 5, rotated -8 degrees
Stone 3 (largest):  24x24, border-radius 6, rotated -12 degrees
```

The stones are spaced so they ascend diagonally with roughly equal visual gaps between them. The increasing rotation adds dynamism without breaking the sense of stability.

## Colour Palette

### Parent Brand: Everyday Arc

The parent brand uses copper and dusty mauve in an alternating pattern (copper, mauve, copper). These colours were chosen specifically to be independent from either product brand, giving Everyday Arc its own identity as the company behind both apps.

| Colour | Hex | Usage |
|--------|-----|-------|
| Copper | `#A67C5B` | Stones 1 and 3 (first and last) |
| Dusty Mauve | `#9A8599` | Stone 2 (middle) |

**On dark backgrounds**, lighter variants are used:

| Colour | Hex | Usage |
|--------|-----|-------|
| Light Copper | `#C9A482` | Stones 1 and 3 on dark backgrounds |
| Light Mauve | `#B8A0B7` | Stone 2 on dark backgrounds |

**Why these colours:**

- **Copper** is warm, grounded, and earthy. It suggests craftsmanship and quiet strength without the corporate feel of gold or the coolness of silver. It feels handmade and human.
- **Dusty Mauve** adds a softness and a hint of depth. It sits between purple's thoughtfulness and grey's calm, creating a colour that feels reflective and unhurried.
- **The alternating pattern** (copper, mauve, copper) creates visual rhythm and prevents the icon from reading as a simple gradient. The middle stone breaks the pattern, making the mark more memorable and giving it a subtle playfulness.

### Opacity progression

The stones use opacity to reinforce the sense of emergence:

| Stone | Light background | Dark background |
|-------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Stone 1 (smallest) | 0.35 | 0.30 |
| Stone 2 (middle) | 0.60 | 0.55 |
| Stone 3 (largest) | 0.90 | 0.85 |

## Typography

### Cormorant (serif, display)

**Used for:** Brand wordmark, all headings, section titles, pull quotes, product names, product taglines

**Weights in use:**
- **400** for content headings (h1, h2, h3), statement text, product taglines (italic)
- **600** for the brand wordmark in the navigation and footer

**Why Cormorant:**

Cormorant is a high-contrast serif with tall, elegant proportions inspired by Claude Garamond's work. It was chosen for several reasons:

1. **It carries weight gracefully.** At 600, the wordmark has real presence alongside the solid geometric stepping stones, without feeling heavy or aggressive. Many serif fonts become clunky at heavier weights. Cormorant stays refined.

2. **True weight range.** Unlike Instrument Serif (the previous choice, which only offered weight 400), Cormorant provides properly designed weights from 300 to 700. This means the brand mark at 600 and content headings at 400 are both purpose-built by the type designer, not simulated through CSS tricks.

3. **It bridges organic and structured.** The letterforms have calligraphic roots (the soft terminals, the gentle stress angle) while maintaining clean, rational proportions. This mirrors the brand's own duality: human warmth expressed through considered design.

4. **Visual cohesion.** Using one serif family throughout (wordmark, headings, taglines) gives the site a unified typographic voice. The weight difference between 600 (brand) and 400 (content) creates natural hierarchy without introducing a second serif.

5. **It reads as calm, not corporate.** The tall ascenders and open counters give Cormorant an airy, unhurried quality. It feels literary and thoughtful, which suits a brand built around psychological safety and gentle tools.

### Inter (sans-serif, body)

**Used for:** Body text, navigation links, buttons, labels, feature lists, form elements

**Weights in use:**
- **300** for lighter supporting text and descriptions
- **400** for standard body copy
- **500** for emphasis within body text, button labels
- **600** for small caps labels, section markers (e.g. "OUR APPROACH")

**Why Inter:**

Inter is a highly legible sans-serif designed specifically for screen use. Its generous x-height and open letterforms make body text comfortable to read at any size. It provides a clean, neutral counterpoint to Cormorant's expressiveness, ensuring the serif always feels like the voice of the brand while the sans-serif handles the practical work of being read.

### The pairing

The contrast between Cormorant and Inter reflects the brand itself: warmth and structure, personality and practicality, the human and the systematic. Cormorant speaks with character. Inter gets out of the way and lets you read. Together they create a typographic system that feels both premium and approachable.
